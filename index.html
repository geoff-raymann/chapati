What about Chapatis and the requirements?

Description

Chapatis are made from wheat flour. The flour is usually powdered to allow easy baking process or whiskying when making other baked foods (Cakes, buns, breads, doughnuts etc) . Chapaties are usually considered a carbohydrate. They form into round shapes. The sizes of the shapes depends on the chef. I usually prefer medium sizes.

People usually say that hot water makes hard chapatis but maybe it is because they never tried using it. I have never seen them make soft chapatis with cold water anyway
Use hot water if you need soft chapatis... I do not know your favourite stew. But mine is Zaire beans. Well prepaired

Ingredients

  • 3 cup-full of wheat flour
  • 2 tablespoonfull of SALT
  • 1 tablespoonfull of powdered sugar
  • 2 topfull of cooking oil
  • 6 cups of water
Use the above measurements to help approximate depending on the number to be served

Cooking and Serving

Instructions

  1. Put the 3 cup-full of wheat flour (remember to consider the number of people to serve and reduce appropriately) in a bowl. Chun with hand to eliminate any trapped air bubbles

  2. Boil water in a cooking can. Add salt and sugar to the hot water and Stir to mix. Add to the flour the water at gradual while mixing until tender. Add 2 topfull of oil and mix until the stickiness is eliminated. Let the dough Cool for 30 minutes
  3. Start dividing the dough into sizeable round ball-like. Put them in a dry surface that is powdered
  4. Roll each ball into flat round shapes. apply oil on one surface then form, first into a ribbon and then a circular shape pressing the centers.
  5. Now heat your frying pan and wet it with oil
  6. Start rolling each ball and put on the pan to first dry. Add oil on each dried surface while turning. Use your cooking spoon to check if both sides and edges are turned brown. Go to the next and the next until all the rolls are cooked.

Serve when Hot with your Favourite Stew.

Some prefer it cut into the stew while others prefer bite-and-scoop method
